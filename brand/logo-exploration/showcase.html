Router Glyph

01

Three agent inputs converge into a command rail. This is the most direct expression of `@all`, point-to-point routing, and the app as a dispatch layer.

routeiconshortlist

Team Orbit

02

A calm team system around a central controller. It is less literal than the terminal marks and works better if the brand should feel more approachable.

teamsoft

Handoff Stack

03

The document is the carrier of context, not decoration. This direction says the product's moat is durable handoff, review, and file-backed work.

docshandoffshortlist

Terminal Relay

04

Literal multi-terminal orchestration. It is highly product-specific, useful for docs and screenshots, but slightly busier at tiny favicon sizes.

terminalliteral

Worktree Fork

05

Parallel branches and review paths. This is the most developer-native mark, good if the product should lean into Git/worktree workflows.

gitparallel

Doc Matrix

06

A modular file grid with one routed outcome. Compact and stable, but a little more abstract unless paired with the product name.

filessystem

Focus Pulse

07

A status-aware mark: focused panel, waiting input, and the user as controller. It aligns well with the current ambient-effects direction.

statusfocusshortlist

Local Mesh

08

Private orchestration inside a bounded local container. Broad and platform-like, with a colder infra tone than the amber variants.

localmesh
Recommended starting point: refine Router Glyph for the app mark, keep Handoff Stack as a docs/product illustration mark, and test Focus Pulse if the brand should lean into "watch the team, intervene when needed."
